Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 21, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how Texas Business Fiber (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information when you visit our website, request Dedicated Internet Access (DIA) quotes, or otherwise interact with our services. Please read this policy carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your personal information and how we will treat it.

1. Introduction

Texas Business Fiber operates a provider-agnostic comparison service that helps businesses across Texas evaluate Dedicated Internet Access options. In the course of providing these services, we may collect and process certain information about you and your organization.

By accessing or using our website or services, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, you should discontinue use of our website and services.

This Privacy Policy applies to information we collect:

  • Through our website and any related landing pages.
  • When you request DIA quotes, contact us, or subscribe to communications.
  • In email, text, and other electronic messages between you and our team.

This policy does not apply to information collected by any third-party websites or services that may be linked from our site, or by carriers and service providers that ultimately deliver connectivity or related services. Those parties have their own privacy policies and practices.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information that identifies, relates to, describes, or is reasonably capable of being associated with you or your organization (“personal information”). The categories of information we may collect include:

2.1 Information You Provide to Us

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when you use our site or interact with us, such as when you:

  • Submit a request for DIA quotes or fiber availability.
  • Complete our contact form or email us.
  • Subscribe to updates, resources, or other communications.

This may include:

  • Contact details such as your name, business email address, phone number, and job title.
  • Business information such as company name, business address, service locations, and industry.
  • Service requirements such as desired bandwidth, redundancy needs, number of sites, and project timelines.
  • Communications including messages you send through forms or via email, and any other information you choose to share.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain technical and usage information, such as:

  • IP address, browser type, and operating system.
  • Device identifiers and device characteristics.
  • Pages visited, time and date of visits, referring and exit pages.
  • Clickstream data and interaction with site elements.

This information is typically collected using cookies, pixels, and similar tracking technologies. It helps us understand how visitors use our site, improve performance, and enhance user experience.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

In connection with providing DIA quotes and related services, we may receive information from:

  • Network carriers and service providers regarding availability, pricing, and serviceability.
  • Public databases and mapping tools for address validation and service qualification.
  • Partners or referral sources who introduce you to our services.

We use this information solely to support your connectivity evaluation and to deliver accurate, relevant options for your locations.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

3.1 Providing and Improving Our Services

  • To qualify your addresses for DIA and related connectivity services.
  • To obtain and present quotes from multiple carriers that can service your locations.
  • To provide guidance, recommendations, and support from initial quote through installation.
  • To operate, maintain, and improve our website and service offerings.

3.2 Communication

  • To respond to your inquiries and requests.
  • To send you information about quotes, service options, timelines, and related updates.
  • To provide administrative information, such as changes to our policies or terms.

3.3 Analytics and Security

  • To analyze website usage and performance, and to understand how visitors interact with our content.
  • To detect, prevent, and address technical issues, fraud, or security incidents.
  • To maintain the safety, security, and integrity of our systems and data.

3.4 Legal and Compliance

  • To comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal processes, or enforceable governmental requests.
  • To enforce our agreements, including our Terms of Service.
  • To protect our rights, privacy, safety, or property, and that of you or others.

4. Sharing and Disclosure of Information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share or disclose information in the following situations:

  • Service Providers and Carriers: We may share necessary information with network carriers, connectivity providers, and other partners solely to obtain quotes, verify serviceability, or coordinate installation and support for your locations.
  • Vendors and Tools: We may use trusted third-party vendors to host our website, process form submissions, provide analytics, or assist with communications. These vendors are permitted to use your information only as necessary to provide services to us.
  • Business Transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as permitted by law, subject to appropriate safeguards.
  • Legal Obligations: We may disclose information when we believe it is necessary to comply with legal obligations, protect our rights, or respond to lawful requests by public authorities.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We may use cookies, pixels, and similar tracking technologies to collect and store certain information when you visit our website.

5.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They help the site recognize your device, remember your preferences, and understand how you interact with content.

5.2 Types of Cookies We May Use

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Required for basic site functionality and security.
  • Performance and Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site so we can improve content and user experience.

5.3 Managing Cookies

Most web browsers are set to accept cookies by default. You can usually choose to set your browser to remove or reject cookies. If you do so, some features of the site may not function as intended.

By continuing to use our website without changing your browser settings, you agree to our use of cookies and similar technologies as described in this policy.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including to provide our services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

The specific retention period may vary based on the nature of the information and the context in which it was collected. When information is no longer needed, we may delete it, anonymize it, or aggregate it for analytical purposes.

7. Data Security

We take reasonable administrative, technical, and physical measures designed to safeguard the information we collect against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.

However, no method of transmission over the internet or method of electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.

8. Your Choices and Rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, such as:

  • The right to access the personal information we hold about you.
  • The right to request correction or update of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • The right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal and contractual obligations.
  • The right to opt out of certain communications from us.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details in the “Contact Us” section below. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

9. Third-Party Links and Services

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, tools, or services that are not operated by us. For example, our contact forms may integrate with external submission or email services.

We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of such third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites or services you visit or use.

10. Children’s Privacy

Our website and services are intended for business and professional users and are not directed to children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If we become aware that we have collected such information, we will take reasonable steps to delete it.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or other factors. When we make changes, we will revise the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.

Your continued use of our website or services after any changes to this policy constitutes your acceptance of the updated terms. We encourage you to review this page periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.
